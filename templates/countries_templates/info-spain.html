Low Emission Zones in Spain

 The Spanish Congress approved the Climate Change Act, requiring municipalities with more than 50,000 inhabitants to implement a Low Emission Zone (LEZ) by 2023. This mandates 149 cities to restrict older vehicles, aiming to reduce pollution and promote cleaner air.
 Spain's LEZs, Emergency Schemes, and Access Regulations include the requirement for vehicles to display windscreen stickers. These stickers, part of the national scheme, are essential for accessing LEZs and can be obtained by Spanish vehicles only.
 However, stickers from other countries, such as Germany and France, are officially accepted by Spanish authorities, allowing foreign vehicles to comply with LEZ regulations. Foreign vehicles must still adhere to the specific emission standards and regulations of the Spanish LEZs. This ensures that all vehicles contribute to the reduction of urban emissions and the improvement of air quality.

 Environmental zones are marked by specific traffic signs.

 To be allowed to enter a ZBE (Low Emission Zone), vehicles must be registered.
 The Spanish emissions sticker is exclusively available for vehicles registered in Spain. However, Spanish authorities officially recognize emission stickers issued by other European countries, requiring foreign vehicles to also comply with the LEZ restrictions.

  •  Distintivo Ambiental B: Petrol: Euro 3. Diesel: Euro 4 and 5.
  •  Distintivo Ambiental C: Petrol: Euro 4, 5, and 6. Diesel: Euro 6.
  •  Distintivo Ambiental ECO: Hybrid and/or gas vehicles that also meet: Petrol: Euro 4, 5, and 6. Diesel: Euro 6.
  •  Distintivo Ambiental Cero: 100% electric, fuel cell, plug-in-hybrid (minimum range 40 km) vehicles.
