Low Emission Zones in France

 France has established several Low Emission Zones (LEZs), known as ZCR (Zones à Circulation Restreinte), in various cities to improve air quality by restricting access to high-emission vehicles.
 Vehicles entering these zones must display a Crit'Air vignette, a sticker that classifies vehicles based on their emissions, with categories ranging from Crit'Air E (electric and hydrogen vehicles) to Crit'Air 5 (the most polluting vehicles).
 These LEZs typically operate from Monday to Friday, between 8 AM and 8 PM. and restrict access to vehicles with higher emission levels, especially during periods of high pollution. The Crit'Air system ensures that only vehicles meeting certain emission standards can enter these zones.
 Exemptions in these French LEZs are limited but may include emergency vehicles and vehicles for disabled persons.
 Non-compliance with LEZ regulations can result in fines, emphasizing the importance of adhering to these measures.
 Additionally, more cities across France are planning to introduce LEZs over time, further expanding the effort to ensure cleaner air and healthier urban environments nationwide.

 The boundaries of the environmental zones are indicated by signs featuring a red circle with the label "Zones à Circulation Restreinte" (Restricted Zone). These signs do not specify which vehicles are permitted or prohibited from entering the zone.

 To enter the Low Emission Zone, each vehicle requires a valid French environmental sticker (Certificat qualité de l'Air). Foreign vehicles area affected as well.

  • Crit'Air 0/E: Green sticker for zero emission electric and hydrogen vehicles.
  • Crit'Air 1: Purple sticker for plug-in hybrid vehicles and Euro 5, 6 petrol vehicles.
  • Crit'Air 2: Yellow sticker for Euro 4 petrol vehicles, and Euro 5, 6 diesel vehicles.
  • Crit'Air 3: Orange sticker for Euro 2, 3 petrol vehicles and Euro 4 diesel vehicles.
  • Crit'Air 4: Burgundy sticker for Euro 3 diesel vehicles.
  • Crit'Air 5: Dark grey sticker for Euro 2 diesel vehicles.
