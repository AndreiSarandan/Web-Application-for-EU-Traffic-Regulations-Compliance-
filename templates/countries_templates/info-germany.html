Low Emission Zones in Germany

 Germany has established a national framework for Low Emission Zones (LEZ) that applies to all motor vehicles except motorcycles. Several cities also impose transit bans on heavy goods vehicles passing through.
 Vehicles entering LEZs must display an "Umweltplakette" registration sticker, which indicates the vehicle's emission category. This sticker is mandatory for all vehicles, including foreign ones, and must be placed on the windshield to show compliance with the emission standards required for entry into the LEZs.  In cities like Berlin, Stuttgart, and Darmstadt, diesel driving bans, referred to as 'Dieselfahrverbot', were previously imposed, requiring vehicles to meet the Euro 6 diesel standard to drive in the affected areas. However, these bans were lifted in 2022 as air quality limits for nitrogen dioxide (NO2) can now be met without these driving bans.

 Environmental zones are marked by specific traffic signs.

 To enter an environmental zone, every vehicle must diplay a valid Umweltplakette (environmental sticker).

  •  Green Sticker: Diesel: Euro 4, 5, 6, or Euro 3 with filter. Petrol: Euro 1 or better, pre-Euro 1 with converter.
  •  Yellow Sticker: Diesel: Euro 3, or Euro 2 with filter. Petrol: Euro 1 or better.
  •  Red Sticker: Diesel: Euro 2, or Euro 1 with filter. Petrol: Euro 1 or better.
  •  No Sticker: Diesel: Euro 1 or worse. Petrol: Without a catalytic converter.
