Low Emission Zones in Bulgaria

 Sofia has an Access Regulation for lorries and a Low Emission Zone (LEZ) that is only active during the winter months.
 In January 2023, the Metropolitan Municipal Council of Sofia adopted an ordinance to create zones with low emissions of harmful substances in the atmospheric air within the territory of the Metropolitan Municipality.
 There will be two LEZs in Sofia, one inside the small ring road and one inside the big ring road, with the scheme active each year from 1 December to 28 February.
 The small ring road LEZ applies to M1 motor vehicles used for passenger transport, with a maximum of nine seats, including the driver's seat, as well as N1 motor vehicles used for goods transport, having a maximum mass not exceeding 3.5 tons.
 Eco-stickers are required for vehicles to enter these zones, with the minimum standards evolving over time.
 Starting from 1 December 2023, Eco sticker I will be required, which corresponds to diesel vehicles meeting Euro 4 standards and petrol vehicles meeting Euro 3 standards.
 From 1 December 2024, both Eco sticker I and II will be necessary, requiring diesel vehicles to meet Euro 6 standards and petrol vehicles to meet Euro 4 standards.
 Finally, from 1 December 2028, vehicles will need to display Eco stickers I, II, and III, indicating compliance with Euro 6a standards for both diesel and petrol vehicles.
