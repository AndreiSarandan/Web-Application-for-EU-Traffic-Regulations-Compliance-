Low Emission Zones in Poland

 Poland has various Access Regulation Schemes, including limited traffic zones, pedestrian areas, LTZ for lorries, and weight restrictions in major cities. Low Emission Zones (LEZ) will be established in Krakow and Warsaw starting from 1 July 2024.
 In Krakow, the LEZ will have two phases. From 1 July 2024, the minimum standards are Euro 1 for petrol/LPG and Euro 2 for diesel vehicles registered before 1 March 2023, and Euro 3 for petrol/LPG and Euro 5 for diesel vehicles registered after that date. From 1 July 2026, the standards will be Euro 3 for petrol/LPG and Euro 5 for diesel vehicles.
 Warsaw's LEZ will also start on 1 July 2024, with the minimum standards being Euro 2 for petrol and Euro 4 for diesel. These standards will tighten over time, reaching Euro 6 for both petrol and diesel by 1 July 2032.
 Other Polish cities, such as Wrocław, Bydgoszcz, Gliwice, Łódź, Zabrze, Rzeszów, and Gdańsk might impose LEZs in the future.
