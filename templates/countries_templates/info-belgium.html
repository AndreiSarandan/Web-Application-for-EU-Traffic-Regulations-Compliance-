Low Emission Zones in Belgium

 Belgium has established three Low Emission Zones (LEZs) in Antwerp, Brussels, and Ghent to improve air quality by restricting access to high-emission vehicles. Notably, stickers are not required for entry into these zones. However, foreign vehicles must be registered, which is free of charge, and this registration applies to Euro 2 petrol and Euro 5 diesel vehicles.
 In Antwerp, the LEZ covers the entire city center and part of Linkeroever, operating 24/7, with restrictions on diesel vehicles below Euro 5 and petrol vehicles below Euro 2 standards.
 Brussels' LEZ encompasses the entire Brussels-Capital Region, also operating 24/7, with similar vehicle restrictions.
 Ghent's LEZ, covering the city center, follows the same operating hours and restrictions. Exemptions across these zones typically include historic vehicles (over 40 years old), emergency vehicles, and vehicles for disabled persons.
