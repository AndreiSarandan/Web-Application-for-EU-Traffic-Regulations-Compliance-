Low Emission Zones in Denmark

 Denmark has implemented Low Emission Zones (LEZs) in four major cities: Aalborg, Aarhus, Copenhagen, and Odense, to enhance air quality by limiting access to high-emission vehicles. In these zones, vehicles must meet at least the Euro 5 emission standard to enter.
 In Aalborg, the LEZ encompasses the central areas of the city, operating 24/7, with strict adherence to the Euro 5 minimum standard for both diesel and petrol vehicles.
 Aarhus' LEZ also covers the central city areas and operates around the clock, enforcing the same Euro 5 minimum standard.
 Copenhagen, the capital city, has one of the most comprehensive LEZs, covering the entire city center and requiring vehicles to meet the Euro 5 standard at all times.
 In Odense, the LEZ includes the central parts of the city, ensuring that only vehicles that meet the Euro 5 standard can access these areas, 24/7.
 Exemptions across these Danish LEZs typically include historic vehicles, emergency services, and vehicles for disabled persons.
